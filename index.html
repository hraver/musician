JERRY GARCIA


This is an homage to the music of Jerry Garcia.

His music, whether solo, with the Grateful Dead
or other bands has always had a wonderful flow to it.

This is Jerry teaching banjo to student in April, 1964. There is no information about the student.

His early music was more bluegrass based and he was an accomplished banjo player, as well other instruments.

Jerry in concert circa 1991.

In 1986, Jerry went into a near fatal diabetic coma for several days, after which he had to re-learn the guitar. The fact that he re-learned his instrument so well is testimony to his skill and passion for his music.

This portrait taken in 1995, not long before Jerry's death.

Old and in the Way was a bluegrass band formed by Garcia with David Grisman, Peter Rowan, Vassar Clements, John Kahn, and Richard Greene. The title album was recorded in 1973 and released in 1975.

The 1969 concert in Woodstock was, reportedly not the best show that the Grateful Dead had put on, there was a lot of rain and ensuing technical problems. It has been said that this was the start of the Dead's psychedelic music. Notwishstanding, the music always had roots in country, folk, blues, etc.
